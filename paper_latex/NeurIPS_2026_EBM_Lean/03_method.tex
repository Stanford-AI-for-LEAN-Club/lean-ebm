\subsection{Energy-Based Model Formulation}

Let $E_\theta(x)$ denote the energy of a tactic sequence $x$ given a proof state.
Lower energy indicates a more promising tactic.
We train $E_\theta$ via contrastive divergence or EBT loss.

\subsection{Langevin Dynamics for Tactic Sampling}

Starting from noise $x_0 \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I)$, we iterate:
\begin{equation}
  x_{t+1} = x_t - \frac{\alpha}{2} \nabla_x E_\theta(x_t) + \sqrt{\alpha}\,\epsilon_t,
  \quad \epsilon_t \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I).
\end{equation}
